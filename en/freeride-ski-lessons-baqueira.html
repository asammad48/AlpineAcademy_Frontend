Why Choose a Freeride Lesson with Alpine Ski Academy?

Freeride is not just skiing virgin snow. It's knowing how to read the terrain, anticipate the line, master your technique in changing conditions and, above all, doing it safely. At Alpine Ski Academy we guide you through every step of your off-piste evolution with a professional, progressive approach adapted to your level.

Terrain Knowledge and Key Areas

We know the best lines and conditions according to the season and snow state.

Recommended Safety Equipment

We strongly recommend the use of ARVA, shovel, probe and ABS backpack as basic safety equipment in any freeride lesson, even within the skiable domain. The ABS backpack, in particular, can be decisive in case of avalanche. We encourage conscious and responsible decision-making as an essential part of freeride practice.

Frequently Asked Questions about Freeride Lessons in Baqueira

What technical level do I need for freeride with Alpine Ski Academy?

To start in our freeride lessons you need to ski confidently on red slopes, maintaining speed control and executing dynamic parallel turns.

No previous experience in virgin snow is necessary, but consolidated technique on slopes is required: good position, terrain adaptation ability and body centering mastery. If you already ski black slopes fluently, you can opt for intermediate or advanced level lessons.

Is ARVA, shovel, probe and ABS backpack mandatory?

Although our lessons take place exclusively within the skiable domain, we strongly recommend using ARVA, shovel, probe and ABS backpack in all freeride sessions.

These elements increase your safety and are part of standard equipment in untracked terrain. If you don't have them, we can guide you to rent or buy them. Specific training should be done with professional guides.
