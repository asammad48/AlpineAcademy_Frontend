Baqueira: the perfect progression to learn step by step

The Baqueira 1800 sector is probably the most complete for a beginner, with a very well-marked progression that allows you to consolidate your technique step by step.

Progression in Baqueira

  1. Beginner conveyor belt behind the nursery: an almost flat area, perfect for the first hour of skiing.
  2. Carreretes 1 and 2 conveyor belts: very easy green slopes where you can start practicing your first wedge turns.
  3. Rabada ski lift: here you work on advanced wedge and the beginning of parallel skiing.
  4. Era Cabana (4-seater detachable chairlift): difficult green slope and very easy blue, ideal for consolidating parallel skiing on longer and more varied routes.

Beret: spaciousness, comfort and reduced ski pass for beginners

The Beret sector is highly valued by beginners for its spaciousness, good visibility and lower crowds.

Progression in Beret

  1. Beginner conveyor belt: wide, flat area perfect for the first contact with skiing.
  2. Beginner lift: provides access to a long, wide green slope that's very comfortable for linking turns and gaining confidence.
  3. Clot der Os (4-seater detachable chairlift): takes you to higher elevations, but without an instructor who knows the route well, you could end up on slopes not suitable for your level.

Additionally, Beret offers a reduced beginner ski pass, ideal for your first day if you're not yet going to explore the rest of the resort.

Bonaigua: ideal for the first hours

The Bonaigua sector has a very comfortable start for beginners:

Progression in Bonaigua

  1. Beginner rope tow: perfect for the first wedge supports.
  2. Two-seater chairlift (intermediate stop): very short and simple green slope, ideal for the first two hours of learning.

From here, the progression becomes more complicated: the next option is Argulls (easy blue slope but long and with traffic from faster skiers). This makes it less suitable for a beginner on their third day, compared to the much more logical progression in Baqueira and Beret.

Expert Tips: Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

  • Not starting with an instructor: the first steps are the foundation of your future as a skier. A bad start creates blocks, fears and bad habits that are very difficult to correct later.
  • Skipping progressions too soon: going up to a blue slope without mastering advanced wedge is one of the most common causes of scares and frustrations.
  • Choosing the wrong time slot: if you only have 2-3 hours or want to avoid crowds, we recommend starting lessons from 1:45 PM. From 1:30 PM there's a reduced pass and the slopes become much emptier.
  • Snow conditions: each sector and each time of day offers different qualities (harder in the morning, softer at midday). At Alpine Ski Academy we always guide our students to the best area at each moment.

Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

Where to rent ski equipment?

In Beret there is a very convenient rental at the foot of the slopes. For more options in Ruda and Vielha, check out our article on ski equipment rental in Baqueira.

Is it better to start in a group or in a private class?

A private class accelerates learning, avoids fears and adapts 100% to your level. For beginners, it's the best investment. Discover more in our article about how to learn to ski in Baqueira.

Conclusion: start your learning safely

Each sector has its charm, but the logical and safe progression is found in Baqueira and Beret. Knowing the right lifts and changing slopes at the right time is key to making your experience fun and without blocks.

At Alpine Ski Academy we are experts in guiding you through each step of that journey. With us you'll learn not only the technique, but also how to move around the resort with confidence and safety.
